About Me

I'm Zoe Fingleton, a junior at Smith College, pursuing a major in Computer Science and a minor in Religion. I am deeply interested, and invested, in the relationship between creator and machine. How do we use technology for work? For play? In what ways do our interactions frame how we go about navigating both online and offline spaces? My primary interest lies in balancing digital ethics with technological advancement, and exploring how the social implications of machine learning work in tandem with progress.

I have experience with research in machine learning, computer vision, and reproducibility. I've developed a novel algorithm for self-supervised learning using a siamese network, built and programmed an autonomous robot capable of navigating mazes and extinguishing fires, read Descartes and Turing alike, worked with international humanitarian groups, hosted a radio show, and conducted extensive research alone, in pairs, and in groups.
