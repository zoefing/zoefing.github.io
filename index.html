About Me

I'm Zoe Fingleton, a sophomore at Smith College, pursuing a major in Computer Science and a minor in Religion. My primary interest lies in exploring the ethical implications of AI integration in data collection and how its application can impact user experience in the digital world.

I have experience with research, data science, machine learning, deep learning, software development, robotics, and data analysis. I've developed a novel algorithm for self-supervised learning using a siamese network, built and programmed an autonomous robot capable of navigating mazes and extinguishing fires, read Descartes and Turing alike, worked with international humanitarian groups, hosted a radio show, and conducted extensive research alone, in pairs, and large groups.
